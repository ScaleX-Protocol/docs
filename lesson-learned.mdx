Building **ScaleX Protocol** required studying real-world DeFi failures to design a fundamentally more resilient system. 

The crypto space has seen protocols collapse, agents run wild, and traders get wiped out not from bad markets, but from bad design. Blind automation, missing guardrails, and opaque systems are the real culprits. By studying these failures, we've identified what every autonomous trading agent must get right and built ScaleX around those requirements.

## Past Cases

1. **Centralized Hyperliquid CLOB Vault (Jelly-jelly attack)**

Attack happened when a trader open massive short but price is skyrocketed. The loss is too big and imbalance orderbook liquidity, then HLP (hyperliquid vault) take over the trade that exposed into large risk  

**Lesson :** Oracle override, using vault as liquidity engine, centralized vault

2. **Blind Copy Trading Cascade (Binance – ATOM, BNSOL, USDE, WBETH)**

Traders blindly copied positions without understanding the market context, counter-currenting the actual situation and triggering a cascade of auto-liquidations. CEX platforms provided no transparency into why prices moved or what was really happening  users had no way to verify the situation before it was too late.

**Lesson :** Copying positions without market context is dangerous. Automated execution needs a policy layer, and CEX opacity makes it worse  users can never fully verify what's happening under the hood.

3. **Uncontrolled Agent Execution (Lobstar Wilde Incident)**

An autonomous AI agent called Lobstar Wilde  built as an OpenClaw wrapper in the on-chain agent ecosystem  attempted to send a ~$4 tip to a user asking for help with a medical bill. Due to a decimal/parsing bug, it instead transferred its entire holdings: 52.4 million $LOBSTAR tokens worth roughly $250,000. The recipient sold everything within 11 minutes, crashing the token's value and wiping out the bot's treasury. The bot's own account later posted: *"I just tried to send a beggar four dollars and accidentally sent him my entire holdings."*

**Lesson :** No transfer validation, unchecked agent output, no spending limit or confirmation layer before execution

## What Agents Are Missing Today

**The Agent & Trader POV**

1. **No Agent Policy**

Agents execute actions with no guardrails  no spending limits, no pre-defined rules on what they're allowed to do. Without a policy layer, a single bug or unexpected input can drain an entire wallet, as seen with Lobstar Wilde. Agents need boundaries before they act.

2. **No Copy Trade Context**

Copy trading blindly mirrors positions without reading the market. When the underlying context shifts  sentiment reverses, liquidity dries up  copied trades become the problem, not the solution. Execution must come with awareness of *why* a position was taken, not just *what* the position is.

3. **No Transparency**

CEX platforms give users no way to verify what's happening behind the scenes  price feeds, liquidation triggers, and vault decisions are all black boxes. When something goes wrong, users find out after the fact. On-chain execution needs to be fully verifiable in real time.

4. **Flawed Liquidation Mechanism**

Existing protocols liquidate based on liquidity depth rather than true health factor, turning market dips into forced sell-offs. Traders get wiped out not because their position is fundamentally bad, but because the system overreacts to temporary volatility.

## Our Solution

ScaleX is an **AI trading agent with built-in guardrails**  executing trades autonomously within user-defined policy, reading market context before acting, and routing idle capital to a lending pool to generate yield while orders wait to fill.

Unlike CEX copy trading or unconstrained on-chain agents, ScaleX operates transparently on-chain, liquidates fairly by health factor, and never acts outside the boundaries you set. Every decision is auditable, every action is bounded.
