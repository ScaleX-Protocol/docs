## üí° Auto Repay (OKX)

**Mechanism:**
Automatically repays borrowed funds once a position is closed or profit is realized.

**Process:**
1. After a margin or leveraged position closes, the system checks outstanding debt.
2. Any available balance or profit is used to repay the borrowed amount.
3. Remaining funds return to the user‚Äôs account.

**Purpose:**
Reduces idle debt and avoids unnecessary interest accumulation.

**Disadvantage:**
- Repayment logic is rigid; users cannot customize how much to repay.
- Can unintentionally reduce liquidity if profits are automatically locked for repayment.

## ‚öôÔ∏è Binance Post Only

**Mechanism:**
Ensures that an order is added to the order book as a **maker order** only.

**Process:**
1. When placing an order, if it would execute immediately, it is cancelled.
2. This guarantees the order provides liquidity instead of taking it.

**Purpose:**
Used by traders to maintain maker status and earn fee rebates while avoiding instant execution.

**Disadvantage:**
- Rebates are only earned **after** the order executes; idle orders earn nothing.
- Orders may get cancelled frequently in fast-moving markets, leading to missed opportunities.

## ‚öñÔ∏è Bybit Dual Win

**Mechanism:**
A structured product that blends **options** and **yield strategies** to offer two potential profit outcomes.

**Process:**
1. User deposits an asset and selects a target price (strike).
2. If the target is reached, funds convert to stablecoins at a premium.
3. If not, the user earns fixed yield on the deposited asset.

**Purpose:**
Allows users to earn yield in both price outcomes ‚Äî ideal for passive or range-bound market conditions.

**Disadvantage:**
- Comes with a **fixed settlement price** and **expiry date**, limiting flexibility.
- Users cannot adjust or exit positions mid-term without penalty.
- Yield and conversion rates depend heavily on market volatility.
