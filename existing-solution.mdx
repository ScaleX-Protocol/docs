## **1. Fluid Protocol – Unified Cross-Margin & Liquidity Layer**

**Mechanism:**  
An on-chain prime-brokerage–style system giving users a single margin account where all assets are pooled together. This unified balance can be used for trading, borrowing, or liquidity actions without juggling multiple accounts.  
However, Fluid’s trading engine still relies on **AMM mechanics**, meaning prices shift with pool reserves and liquidity depth rather than pure market supply/demand like a CLOB.

**Process:**

1. Users deposit assets into a unified margin account.  
2. All assets immediately contribute to collateral and borrowing power.  
3. Users trade, borrow, or interact with liquidity from the same internal pool.  
4. A real-time cross-margin engine manages combined position risk.

**Purpose:**  
To simplify the user experience and boost capital efficiency by merging spot, margin, and liquidity into one system. Idle balances are minimized because everything contributes to a single collateral pool.

**Disadvantages:**

* **AMM-based execution** → slippage and liquidity depth issues still apply.  
* Borrowed funds and actions remain inside Fluid’s internal environment.  
* Risk logic depends on Fluid’s internal margin engine.  
* Does not generate yield for idle limit orders or inactive trading capital.

## **2. Binance / Bybit - Dual Investment**

**Mechanism:**  
A structured product that blends yield generation with options-like behavior. Users earn returns while setting a conditional buy or sell price. If the market reaches the strike at settlement, the asset converts automatically.

**Process:**  
1. User deposits an asset and selects a target (strike) price.  
2. If the target is reached, the asset converts at that price.  
3. If not reached, the user earns a fixed yield.

**Purpose:**  
Allows users to earn yield or accumulate assets passively.

**Disadvantage:**  
Fixed expiry, no flexibility mid-term, and yield is tied to volatility.

---

## **3. Fidelity - Margin Loans**

**Mechanism:**  
Traditional brokerage borrowing where users pledge portfolio assets to access leverage.

**Process:**  
1. Pledge eligible securities.  
2. Borrow cash based on collateral value.  
3. Interest accrues daily until repayment.

**Purpose:**  
Provides liquidity without selling existing holdings.

**Disadvantage:**  
Manual repayment, fixed-rate logic, no crypto support, and no on-chain transparency.

---

## **4. OKX - Auto-Borrow & Auto-Repay**

**Mechanism:**  
Automatically borrows when an order exceeds available funds, and repays once the position is closed.

**Process:**  
1. User places an order larger than the available balance.  
2. System auto-borrows the shortfall.  
3. Borrowed amount is auto-repaid when closing the position.

**Purpose:**  
Simplifies margin trading by reducing manual steps.

**Disadvantage:**  
Interest starts instantly, users can’t control repayment logic, and all assets must remain in centralized custody.

---

## **5. Glow Finance - Margin & Yield Engine**

**Mechanism:**  
On-chain margin account where assets earn yield while serving as collateral. Borrowed funds can only be used inside the Glow ecosystem (e.g., for trading or restaking).

**Process:**  
1. Deposit assets → earn yield + count as collateral.  
2. Borrow supported assets for internal actions (trading, restaking).  
3. Repay borrowed amount or face liquidation if health drops.

**Purpose:**  
Combines yield generation and leverage in a unified environment.

**Disadvantage:**  
Internal-only usage and collateral liquidation risk.
